Where and How Americans Die Abroad

By year and category

Since 2003, the U.S. State Department has kept complete annual records of where and how U.S. citizens die unnatural deaths in foreign countries. A number of categories exist for deaths such as homicide, suicide and motor vehicle. Toggle between the years and categories to see where and how Americans die abroad.

Total deaths abroad

2003-2014

Throughout the past decade Mexico has had the most American fatailities abroad, 2730, with Thailand coming in a distant second at 326. Costa Rica has the third highest total with 315 and Iraq, Germany and Haiti all had 252. The largest categories in Mexico were motor vehicle accidents and homicides.

Deaths per capita

Per 100,000 US visitors

More Americans visit Mexico than any other country in the world. Mexico may also have the highest total death count for Americans abroad, but when compared to the total visitors in 2014, Thailand has a much higher death rate per 100,000 American visitors as does all of Asia, which includes the Middle East. Europe, which includes the Ukraine and Eastern Bloc countries, has a relatively low per capita death rate, as does Mexico, in comparison.
